My Recent Work

In this study I show Simulations of protostar-driven photoionization in Herbig-Haro Jets. we compare two different evolutionary simulations, corresponding to a high luminosity case ( $ L_{X} = 10^{32} erg/s \quad $, upper movie ) and a low luminosity case ($ L_{X} = 10^{28} erg/s \quad$ lower movie). While the latter does not produce any noticeable difference in the amount of ionization when compared to the purely radiative case (see paper [Z.ahmane et al, 2020] ), the former results in an appreciably larger ionization rate. Close to the central object we now observe almost $ \approx 50 \%$ ionization degree, lowering to $\approx 10 \% \quad$ at large distances. Ions can survive large distance propagation owing to the rather long recombination scale.
